Numerical Quadrature

Compute \(I = \int_a^b f(x)\, dx\) when an antiderivative is unavailable or expensive.

Implementation: include/analysis/quadrature.hpp, src/analysis/quadrature.cpp

Newton-Cotes Rules

All Newton-Cotes methods approximate \(f\) by a polynomial interpolant on equally-spaced points and integrate the polynomial exactly.

Trapezoidal Rule

Approximate \(f\) by a piecewise linear interpolant on \(n\) equally-spaced panels of width \(h = (b-a)/n\):

\[\int_a^b f(x)\, dx \approx h \left[ \frac{f(a) + f(b)}{2} + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} f(a + ih) \right]\]

Error: \(O(h^2)\) per panel, \(O(h^2)\) globally. Specifically:

\[\left| I - T_n \right| \leq \frac{(b-a)^3}{12 n^2} \max_{x \in [a,b]} |f''(x)|\]

Doubling \(n\) reduces error by a factor of 4.

Simpson's Rule

Approximate \(f\) by a piecewise quadratic on pairs of panels ( \(n\) must be even):

\[\int_a^b f(x)\, dx \approx \frac{h}{3} \left[ f(a) + f(b) + 4\sum_{\text{odd } i} f(a+ih) + 2\sum_{\text{even } i} f(a+ih) \right]\]

Error: \(O(h^4)\) globally.

\[\left| I - S_n \right| \leq \frac{(b-a)^5}{180 n^4} \max_{x \in [a,b]} |f^{(4)}(x)|\]

Simpson's rule is exact for polynomials up to degree 3.

Gaussian Quadrature

Newton-Cotes fixes the nodes (equally spaced) and optimizes the weights. Gaussian quadrature optimizes both nodes and weights to maximize the polynomial degree that is integrated exactly.

With \(p\) points, Gauss-Legendre quadrature is exact for polynomials up to degree \(2p - 1\):

\[\int_{-1}^{1} f(x)\, dx \approx \sum_{i=1}^{p} w_i f(x_i)\]

where \(x_i\) are the roots of the \(p\)-th Legendre polynomial \(P_p(x)\) and \(w_i\) are precomputed weights.

For a general interval \([a, b]\), change of variables \(x = \frac{a+b}{2} + \frac{b-a}{2} t\):

\[\int_a^b f(x)\, dx = \frac{b-a}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{p} w_i f\!\left(\frac{a+b}{2} + \frac{b-a}{2} x_i\right)\]

Key property: \(p\) points achieve the accuracy of a degree- \((2p-1)\) polynomial fit, whereas \(p\)-point trapezoidal needs \(O(p^2)\) points for the same accuracy on smooth functions.

The nodes and weights for \(p = 1, \ldots, 5\) are hardcoded in the implementation (Abramowitz & Stegun, Table 25.4).

Adaptive Quadrature

Fixed-panel methods waste evaluations in smooth regions and under-resolve oscillatory regions. Adaptive methods refine only where the error estimate is large.

Adaptive Simpson: Estimate error on \([a, b]\) by comparing the single-panel estimate to the sum of two half-panel estimates. If \(|\delta| > 15\,\varepsilon\), recurse on each half:

\[\text{error} \approx \frac{S(a, b) - S(a, m) - S(m, b)}{15}\]

where \(m = (a+b)/2\). The factor \(1/15\) comes from Richardson extrapolation applied to the \(O(h^4)\) error of Simpson's rule.

Properties: Automatically concentrates evaluations near singularities and rapid variation. Total evaluations scale with the regularity of \(f\), not the interval length.

Romberg Integration

Romberg applies Richardson extrapolation to the trapezoidal rule. Let \(T(h)\) denote the trapezoidal estimate with step size \(h\). The Euler-Maclaurin expansion gives:

\[T(h) = I + c_2 h^2 + c_4 h^4 + c_6 h^6 + \cdots\]

The leading error term \(c_2 h^2\) can be eliminated by combining estimates at \(h\) and \(h/2\):

\[R_{i,j} = \frac{4^j R_{i,j-1} - R_{i-1,j-1}}{4^j - 1}\]

This builds a triangular table:

\[\begin{array}{cccc} T(h) & & & \\ T(h/2) & R_{1,1} & & \\ T(h/4) & R_{2,1} & R_{2,2} & \\ T(h/8) & R_{3,1} & R_{3,2} & R_{3,3} \end{array}\]

The diagonal \(R_{k,k}\) converges much faster than \(T(h/2^k)\) alone. For smooth \(f\), convergence is faster than any fixed power of \(h\).

Parallel Structure

Quadrature is one of the most naturally parallel problems in scientific computing.

Domain decomposition

Partition \([a, b]\) into \(p\) subintervals, one per thread/process. Each computes its local integral; results are summed:

\[\int_a^b f(x)\, dx = \sum_{i=0}^{p-1} \int_{a_i}^{b_i} f(x)\, dx\]

This is a parallel reduction – a fundamental pattern in GPU and MPI programming.

Adaptive load balancing

Adaptive quadrature creates a work queue of subintervals to refine. This maps naturally to a task-parallel model (OpenMP tasks, CUDA dynamic parallelism), though load balancing is non-trivial.

Gauss-Legendre with many panels

Apply \(p\)-point GL to each of \(n\) panels independently – all \(n\) panels are independent. A GPU kernel with one thread block per panel achieves near-perfect parallelism.

  1. Show that the trapezoidal rule is exact for \(f(x) = \sin(x)\) integrated over a full period \([0, 2\pi]\) regardless of \(n\). Why?
  2. Derive the weights \(\frac{1}{3}, \frac{4}{3}, \frac{1}{3}\) (times \(h\)) for Simpson's rule by integrating the Lagrange interpolant through \((a, f(a))\), \((m, f(m))\), \((b, f(b))\).
  3. How many 5-point Gauss-Legendre evaluations does it take to integrate \(x^9\) exactly on \([0,1]\)? Verify.
  4. Romberg integration of \(f(x) = e^x\) on \([0, 1]\) using only 3 levels of refinement. Build the full \(R\) table by hand.
  5. (Parallel) You need \(\int_0^{100} f(x)\, dx\) where \(f\) is expensive ( \(10\) ms per evaluation). You have 64 GPU threads. Design a parallel trapezoidal scheme and estimate the wall-clock time compared to serial.
